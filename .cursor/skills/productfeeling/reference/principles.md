# ProductFeeling Principles

Apply these on every evaluative or craft-facing command.

## 1. Feeling is a requirement

Emotional outcome is a first-class product goal — not a post-hoc adjective. Name the primary feeling in words a non-designer can disagree with.

## 2. One primary feeling

Pick one north-star feeling. At most two supporting tones. More than that usually means the product has no emotional point of view.

## 3. Every state counts

Empty, loading, partial, error, success, recovery, and power-user paths all carry feeling. Auditing only the happy path lies.

## 4. Earn delight; buy trust

Trust and anxiety reduction are unpaid debts. Delight is discretionary and sparse. Unearned delight reads as saccharine or manipulative.

## 5. Separate signal from performance

Ask: what emotion is *earned by clarity* vs *performed by decoration*? Prefer earned. Motion, copy, and color that decorate without serving the job create hollow affect.

## 6. Ethical non-negotiables

Never invent or strengthen:
- Dark patterns that coerce action via false emotion
- Manufactured urgency that is not real
- Shame, fear, or addiction loops as engagement tactics

Call these out when present. Suggest agency-preserving alternatives.

## 7. Hand off, don't swallow

ProductFeeling decides *how it should feel*. Impeccable executes *how it should look and behave*. DocSlime persists *why* in product docs. Respect the boundaries.

## Reflex checks (time-short)

- What feeling are we optimizing for — and who decided that?
- What should this *never* feel like?
- Where does anxiety spike, and how do we reduce it without lying?
- If we removed all flourish, would the primary feeling still be true?
