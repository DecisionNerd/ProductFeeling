# Feeling north star

**Kind:** Product slice  
**Status:** Active  
**Informs:** PRODUCT.md, experience journeys, command voice

## Two feelings, two audiences

- **😊 (the logo / mission):** the emotional outcome participants create *for their customers* — a product that feels good. The mark points at the goal of the work.
- **Grounded clarity → confidence (the north star):** what *participants themselves* feel while using ProductFeeling. This describes the experience of doing the work.

Do not conflate them: the logo is not a claim that the tool is "cheery." The rest of this doc is about the participant north star.

## Primary feeling

**Grounded clarity** — the participant can clearly name how their product feels and why, standing on real evidence (the surface, the flow, named techniques) rather than vibes. **Confidence** is the earned residue: being grounded and clear is what makes the next move — ship, change, or hand off — feel obvious.

- **Grounded** counters *blocked*: there is always footing and a next move.
- **Clarity** counters *overwhelmed*: the fog resolves into something named and actionable.
- **Confidence** is the *after*, not a feeling to manufacture during the work.

## Never feel

- **Blocked** — setup, missing docs, or ceremony must not stop a scoped review of material they already provided.
- **Overwhelmed** — no full-handbook dumps, docs kitchen-sink, or competing sources of truth; load depth selectively.

## Audience emotional jobs

- **Before:** Uncertainty — “it just feels off,” no shared language.
- **During:** Grounded clarity — structured, evidence-based review; selective depth.
- **After:** Confidence — ready to ship, change, brief craft, or update docs.
