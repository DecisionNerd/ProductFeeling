# Time to Value


Help people reach their first meaningful outcome with the least unnecessary effort.

## What it is

The elapsed time and effort between a user’s first meaningful touchpoint and experiencing a benefit they came for. It includes waiting, setup, decisions, and uncertainty—not just screen count.

## Why it works

New users are evaluating whether the product can help them. A focused path to a real outcome builds confidence; premature complexity, irrelevant setup, and empty states make the promise harder to judge.

## When to use it

- During onboarding and first-use activation
- When introducing a capability with a delayed or complex payoff
- When research shows people leave before reaching a meaningful outcome

## Do

- Define the first meaningful outcome from user research, not internal feature priority
- Remove or defer steps that do not enable that outcome
- Use Setup Defaults, Sandbox Experience, and Progressive Disclosure to create a credible first result

## Don't

- Optimise only for speed if users need context to act confidently
- Force account details, permissions, or configuration before they are relevant
- Substitute a polished tour for an actual experience of value

## Founder Tip

Measure the path to the first outcome users can recognise in their own words. That is your real time to value.

## Make It Yours

1. What outcome causes a new user to think, “this is useful for me”?
2. Which required steps directly enable that outcome, and which merely serve the business?
3. Where do people wait, hesitate, repeat work, or face a blank state?
4. What safe default, sample, or preview could make value tangible sooner?
5. How will you test whether users reach and recognise that outcome?
