# Shareability


Help users share a meaningful outcome when they want to tell its story.

## What it is

Shareability designs a voluntary, user-owned way to export or post an achievement, creation, result, or insight. The shared item should communicate the user’s accomplishment or taste first; the product’s role is to make that expression easy and accurate.

## Why it works

People often share work that helps them celebrate progress, connect with a community, or explain something useful. A well-designed shareable artefact carries context and gives users control over what leaves the product. Unlike **Referral**, it does not require an invitation loop or reward.

## When to use it

- After a genuine **Success Moment**, meaningful milestone, or completed creation
- When a result is useful or understandable outside the product: a chart, playlist, image, summary, or artefact
- When an existing community practice gives the user a relevant audience for the outcome

## Do

- Let users preview, edit, and remove personal or sensitive details before sharing
- Create accessible, accurate formats with context that makes sense beyond the product
- Keep branding modest and preserve the user’s ownership of the story

## Don’t

- Interrupt a task with share prompts before the user has experienced value
- Manufacture public comparison, streak pressure, or embarrassment to drive posting
- Publish automatically, prefill public audiences, or make sharing a condition of receiving an outcome

## Founder Tip

The strongest share card makes someone look good because it reflects something real they did—not because it turns them into your billboard.

## Make It Yours

1. To earn the moment:
   - What result would a user be proud, relieved, or genuinely pleased to show?
   - Does it reflect real effort, insight, taste, or progress?
2. To preserve ownership:
   - Can the user decide exactly what is included, named, and omitted?
   - Would the shared item still feel like theirs with the brand removed?
3. To make it useful elsewhere:
   - Does someone outside the product understand the result and its context?
   - Are image descriptions, readable text, and accessible alternatives available?
4. To choose timing:
   - Does this follow a completed outcome rather than interrupt work in progress?
   - Is the prompt easy to dismiss permanently or defer?
5. To protect trust:
   - Could this expose sensitive data, location, collaborators, or private performance?
   - Are we inviting expression, or creating social pressure to perform?
