# Referral


Help satisfied users invite people who will genuinely benefit, with a clear and respectful path for everyone.

## What it is

Referral is a product-supported invitation loop: an existing user recommends the product to someone in their network, often because collaboration, access, or a relevant reward creates value for both people. It is distinct from **Shareability**, which centres on sharing an achievement or output.

## Why it works

A recommendation can carry context that paid acquisition cannot: why the product helped and who else might benefit. Referral works when the sender can make a credible recommendation and the recipient receives a clear, honest offer—not when users are pushed to mine their contacts.

## When to use it

- After a clear **Success Moment** or sustained value, not before the product has earned advocacy
- When inviting another person directly improves the product, such as shared workspaces or collaborative artefacts
- When a recipient-facing benefit is real, understandable, and appropriate to the product’s value

## Do

- Explain what the recipient receives, what the sender receives, and when either reward is earned
- Make sharing optional, targeted, and easy to stop; let users choose a channel rather than importing contacts by default
- Place invitations where collaboration or a completed outcome makes the recommendation naturally relevant

## Don’t

- Preselect contacts, send invitations without review, or make referral access a condition of core use
- Promise rewards with hidden thresholds, expiry traps, or unclear eligibility
- Ask users to recommend a product before they have enough experience to stand behind it

## Founder Tip

Design the invitation so a user would be comfortable sending it to one person they respect. If it feels embarrassing or vague, the loop is not ready.

## Make It Yours

1. To earn advocacy:
   - What outcome makes a user confident enough to recommend us?
   - Would they be able to explain the benefit in their own words?
2. To identify the recipient:
   - Who specifically gains value from joining or collaborating?
   - Is this invitation useful to them, rather than merely useful to us?
3. To make the offer clear:
   - What does each person receive, and what action triggers it?
   - Can both people understand the terms before sharing?
4. To reduce social pressure:
   - Can the sender edit the message, choose the channel, or decline without penalty?
   - Are we avoiding contact import and repeated prompts by default?
5. To evaluate quality:
   - Do referred users reach meaningful value and remain by choice?
   - Are complaints or low-quality invitations telling us the placement is wrong?
