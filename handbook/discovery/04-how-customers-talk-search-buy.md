# How Customers Talk, Search, and Buy

Language, watering holes, and the path to payment—each shapes surfaces you will ship.

## What it is

- **Voice of the customer (VoC)** — their vocabulary for problem, category, and outcome (interviews, tickets, calls, reviews, communities). Verbatim phrases are how people recognise a product is for them.
- **Solution-seeking behaviour** — where they look when a need state activates: queries in *their* words, peers, communities, review sites, app stores, AI assistants.
- **Buying journey** — from problem to payment: who is involved, evidence required, comparisons, disqualifiers, what makes risk survivable. Consumer paths may be solo and short; business paths often involve a **buying committee**.

## Why it works

- **Talk** → recognition. Mirrored language produces *being understood*; internal jargon is cognitive [friction](../concepts/04-friction.md).
- **Search** → arrival state. Channel encodes [awareness](02-need-states.md); one landing for all arrivals wastes that signal.
- **Buy** → [calibrated trust](../concepts/11-calibrated-trust.md) under peak doubt. Without their real evaluation process, checkout supplies generic persuasion where specific reassurance was needed.

## When to use it

- Before writing homepage, onboarding, or paywall copy
- Choosing where [Referral](../ttps/referral.md) and [Shareability](../ttps/shareability.md) matter more than ads
- Mapping B2B evidence packs (security, case study, champion kit)
- Instrumenting pricing and checkout doubt

## Do

- Mine language before writing any; cluster by need state; note words they never use
- Reconstruct the last real search: typed what, asked whom, trusted which answer
- Map the decision and its politics: evidence, disqualifiers, who else must say yes
- De-risk at the decision: trial ([Sandbox Experience](../ttps/sandbox-experience.md)), transparent pricing, easy exit ([Graceful Exit](../ttps/graceful-exit.md)), truthful proof
- Name UI, docs, and events in customer vocabulary; preserve arrival context through the funnel ([Deep-link](../ttps/deep-link.md))

## Don't

- Brainstorm taglines without a VoC corpus
- Manufacture urgency at the moment of peak doubt
- Strip campaign/referrer context at signup
- Equip a champion with nothing they can use in the meeting you are not in

## Founder Tip

If your category jargon and their words diverge, close the gap in *their* favour—recognition beats education at the first glance.

## Make It Yours

1. **Harvest 20 verbatims** — cluster by need state; pick phrases for headline tests.
2. **Last search story** — for three ICP interviews.
3. **Buyer evidence list** — what moves them; what disqualifies instantly.
4. **Doubt instruments** — pricing returns, checkout step abandon, trial-to-paid lag.

## Insights & Metrics

1. **VoC reuse** — Shipped headlines/CTAs that reuse a harvested phrase ÷ New customer-facing strings in period.
2. **Search–arrival fit** — Sessions where landing matches inferred awareness from channel.
3. **Buy doubt signals** — Repeat pricing visits and checkout abandon step (fix is usually missing reassurance, not a bigger discount).

## Behind the Data

- Would a customer recognise themselves in your homepage in under five seconds?
- Where does peer trust outrank your content?
- What burned them last time they bought something like this?
