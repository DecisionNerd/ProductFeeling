# Continue learning

This handbook is a starting system, not a closed canon. Prefer **primary sources and durable article libraries** over one-off blog posts when you go deeper. Organise what you learn by *theme*—confidence, recovery, trust, agency—not by source.

## Themes to chase

When researching or asking another agent to synthesise further, organise findings into themes—not source-by-source summaries:

1. Foundations of emotion in product design
2. Human psychological needs (autonomy, competence, relatedness, trust)
3. Emotional journeys across the product lifecycle
4. Designing for confidence instead of confusion
5. Recovery from errors, uncertainty, and failure
6. AI-specific emotional interaction patterns
7. Trust, transparency, and ethical persuasion
8. Community and belonging
9. Product personality, tone, and communication
10. Case studies from leading software products
11. Actionable principles and reusable patterns
12. Metrics and evaluation methods for emotionally aware products

That mix should keep learning grounded in established UX research, behavioural science, and modern AI product design—rather than anecdotal growth tips alone.
